 Why should I foster a pet ?

As a foster parent, you will be able to love, care for, and enjoy to get know a pet . And you will be doing a wonderful thing – giving these loving animals a chance to a new life.

What will I need to do to be a foster parent?

Foster homes are responsible for feeding, exercising, socialising, grooming, reinforcing basic obedience instructions, studying and evaluating overall behaviour and temperament, and, of course, providing patience, love, and security to the fostered cat or dog on a regular basis. It's important to remember that all of the animals are rescued, and their behaviour might be unpredictable. Prepare yourself for some initial adjustment challenges. Being in a new scenario can be stressful for both you and your pet. Wait patiently. We will offer advice and encouragement if the going gets tough.

What if I want to travel?

If you are fostering and want to go away for a weekend or take a vacation then there are many foster homes available that can take care of your pets as you might had and there might be some charges for same.

How long does the adoption process take?

The adoption process usually does not take more than 2 days but depends on many different scenarios

I’d Like To Foster. What Do I Do Next?

If you are interested in fostering, please complete and submit a volunteer application. You will need to complete a volunteer orientation and volunteer at the shelter before becoming a foster volunteer.

What if it doesn’t work out with my new pet?

While we always recommend giving your new pet significant time to adjust to its new environment, if your new pet does not work out for any reason.We'd suggest you should talk with the present foster parents before adopting and get familiar with pets temperament, habits and comfort and dis-comfort zones etc. Also you can request the present foster parents regarding ( if things doesn't workout with the pet) and take the next step to adoption accordingly.
